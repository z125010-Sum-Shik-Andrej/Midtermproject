Favourite Books

1. Crime and Punishment

Author: Fyodor Dostoyevski

This profound psychological novel follows Rodion Raskolnikov, a destitute student who grapples with guilt and redemption after committing a grave crime. His internal turmoil and philosophical reasoning as he interacts with characters like the compassionate Sonia and the insightful Porfiry explore themes of morality, suffering, and the possibility of salvation.

2. 1984

Author: George Orwell

A dystopian tale of Winston Smith's rebellion against a totalitarian regime that controls truth and suppresses free thought under Big Brother's watchful eye. As Winston pursues forbidden love with Julia and uncovers contradictions in Party doctrines, he confronts the terrifying machinery of propaganda and psychological manipulation.

3. Animal Farm

Author: George Orwell

An allegorical novella portraying a group of farm animals' uprising and the corrupting influence of power as their leaders become indistinguishable from the humans they overthrew. Through the rise and fall of leadership figures like Napoleon and Snowball, Orwell satirizes the betrayal of revolutionary ideals and the dangers of totalitarianism.

4. Dr Jekyll and Mr Hyde

Author: Robert Louis Stevenson

An exploration of duality, as Dr. Henry Jekyll's experiments unleash his sinister alter ego, Mr. Edward Hyde, revealing the darkness within human nature. Stevenson's suspenseful narrative examines Victorian anxieties about identity, repression, and the inevitable consequences when one's darker impulses take control.
